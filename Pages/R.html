INTRO

Genetics can affect the ways we live on the daily, but what does it affect? How are these genes passed through generations?




GENETIC TRANSMISSION

Inherited genes within families are transferred during Vertical Genetic Transmission (VGT) directly via sperm/egg. VGT is the most fundamental way in which DNA is transferred. Vertical gene transmission can only take place by sexual reproduction. Humans have used genetic transmission to produce human children and genetically modify crops that we use. GMO crops can be produced by intertwining a male and female plant, causing them to produce offspring holding desired genetic traits like pest resistance or higher yield.




WHAT GENETICS TELLS US ABOUT OUR FAMILY AND CHOICES

Genetics seem to have some sort of influence on the choices we make in our lives. But what can be predisposed by genetics? Based on research taken by comparison between relatives and populations of low genetic variation, a person’s genetics can determine parts of personality, taste, relationships, and activity. Traits like introversion and creativity stem from genetics as well as taste preferences like sweet or savory. Relationships can be regulated by oxytocin-related genes determining happiness. Genetics can draw a person to an activity whether they are skilled or not. All in all, genetics affects a preference, yet not completely. Environmental factors also contribute to human development, sometimes even rejecting predisposed behaviors. Our choices may be connected to our lineage but it doesn't mean we are stuck to it.
